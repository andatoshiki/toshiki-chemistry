# Daily Video 1 Notes-Moles and Molar Mass

## 1-1 Concepts

- **Moles**: Particles can not be counted or measured directly via mass, so we use mass in Chemistry for mass calculation.

- ![](assets/2022-08-22-09-38-07-image.png)
  - $6.02^{*} 10^{23}$ is the number of things in a mole, hence all 4 samples contain **one mole** of particles.
  - All 4 samples contain the same number of particles $6.02\times10^{23}$.
  - **The mass of each sample is equal to the formula mass of the substance**.
  - s particles in units of grams, this is the mass of one **mole or molar mass**.
  
  | Formula      | $\mathrm{C}_{12} \mathrm{H}_{22} \mathrm{O}_{11}$ | $\mathrm{NaCl}$ | $\mathrm{C}$ | $\mathrm{CuSO_4}$ |
  | ------------ | ------------------------------------------------- | --------------- | ------------ | ----------------- |
  | Formula mass | 342.3 u                                           | 58.4 u          | 12.0 u       | 159.6 u           |
  | Molar mass   | 342.3 g/mol                                       | 58.4 g/mol      | 12.0 g/mol   | 159 g/mol         |
  
- While the samples all contain the same number of particles, the mass of each sample is different. Why? **Because the constituent particles in each sample are different.**
  - **constituent particles** is the simplest & smallest unit through which any substance is made.
  
- When we write "formula mass", we usually mean the mass associated with 1 mole of such a formula; i.e. Avogadro's number of such a formula. Molar mass is the same thing: Avogadro's number of whatever stuff you have. The ability to successfully calculate equivalent masses of compounds is vital to success in Chemistry.

## 1-2 Molar Mass/Formula Mass Calculation

### 1-2-1 Example 1

What is the molar/formula mass of Sucrose, $\mathrm{C}_{12} \mathrm{H}_{22} \mathrm{O}_{11}$

1. Count atoms in chemical formula
   - $12 \mathrm{C}, 22 \mathrm{H}, 11 \mathrm{O}$
   
2. Find atomic mass of each element on the periodic table

   - $\mathrm{C}$-12.01u, $\mathrm{H}$-1.01 u, $\mathrm{O}$-16.0 0u

3. Multiply atoms by atomic mass to find mass per element.

   - $\mathrm{C}$-12*12.01u=16.00 u
   - $\mathrm{H}$-22*1.01u=22.2 u
   - $\mathrm{O}$-11*16.00u=176.00 u
